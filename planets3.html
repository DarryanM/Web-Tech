JUPITER

The largest planet in our solar system with a diameter of 88.846 miles / 142.984 kilometers. It is about 11 times bigger than Earth. Jupiter has a distance of 779 million kilometers, or 484 million miless away from the Sun. Jupiter's surface is mostly swirling gasses and liquids. Jupiter is made up of 90% hydrogen and 10% helium. A similar composition to the sun. Jupiter has 79 moons and has 3 faint, dark, and narrow planetary rings which is made up of tiny rock fragments and dust. Jupiter takes 10 hours to complete a rotation and 11.86 years (4,333 days) to orbit the Sun at a speed of 29,236 mph or 13.07 km/s. Jupiter has centries-old storms, some of which are bigger than the earth.

SATURN


The planet with the rings.The 5th planet and second largest planet which is 1.4 billion kilometers / 886 million miles away from the Sun. As a result it takes sunlight 80 minutes to get to Saturn. Saturn is almost 9.5 times bigger than Earth with a diameter of 74,897 miles (120,500 kilometers). Just like Jupiter, Saturn is made up of hydogen and helium. One day on Saturn takes 10.7 hours and one full orbit around the Sun takes 29.4 Earth years (10,756 Earth days). At Last count Saturn had 146 moons attached to its orbit. The rings are made up of comets, asteroids, or shattered moon fragments that got caughtin its powerful gravitaional force.
